% Voter Candidate Range Profiles
% ===================================================

In this section we explore the VCR profiles domain and describe some of its properties.
The main goal is to compare the size of the domain with other well known domains
such as VR and CR. 

In the second set of results,
we present visualizations of our ABC rules under several 
distributions of the ideal points
and several strategies for choosing the voters’ or candidates’radii
We obtain several high-level conclusions. 
For example, we find that forcing the voters to approve a given number of candidates leads 
to unap-pealing results, or that AV might have some deficiencies as a rule 
to choose individually excellent candidates.

\section{Domain analysis}

We have performed several experiments, in each of them we check how many profiles have
the VCR property but not VR neither CR. 
For the first one we focus on the small size elections, and 
for the second experiment we employ several strategies to generate random, larger profiles with the VCR property.

\section{How many VCR profiles are not VR neither CR?}

\subsection{Experiment 1 - Small Profiles}
For the small profiles we performed a complete search of the domain. 
We checked the elections with the following sizes ($M$ candidates $N$ voters):
$[(3,3), (4,4), (5,5), (4,6), (6,4)]$.
The results as seen in Figure \ref{fig:small-statistics-barchart} show that
VCR domain is relatively large, however only limited number of profiles
are not VR nor CR.

\subsubsection{Experiment 2 — large profiles}
For the larger size of the profiles we generated a suitable number of random VCR profiles
and again checked how many of them are not VR nor CR.
To create random VCR profile we generated
points $x_a$ and radii $r_a$ for every agent $a \in C \cup V$
and then created preference matrix based on the VCR definition.

Additionally, to have a broader view of the domain the experiment is 
split into sub-experiments based on the different random distributions
(WIP: Czy distributions jest ok?) used to generate profiles.
