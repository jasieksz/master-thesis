Elections are ubiquitous element of our society in one form or another.
For example choosing members of parliament, winner of a competition.
In a day-to-day activities we need to decide on a meeting time or select recommended movies in a streaming service.
Each of these choices may require different type of election and scoring rule,
based on the underlying properties of the choice (i.e. voters should be represented proportionally by members of a government)

In this work I am going to focus on the \textit{Approval Elections}, that is where each voter provides a subset of candidates that he approves of and disapproves the rest.
In general many interesting voting rules are known to be NP-hard, however become polynomial solvable under some restrictions on the structure of the preferences.

\cite{DBLP:journals/corr/ElkindL15} introduced a new one-dimensional domain restriction (VR / CR) and showed relation with existing ones (SP, SC and others).
\cite{falisz} generalized the model (VCR) and explored two-dimensional case.

The goal of this work is to explore properties of the one-dimensional VCR model and find polynomial algorithms for detection and scoring rules.
