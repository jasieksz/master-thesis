\subsection{AV-CC-DV for Single Peaked ~(VR = CI = row C1P = $r_c=0$)}

Algorithm from \cite{control-sp-shield-faliszewski-hemaspaandra}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item kandydat $p$ na osi, pozostali na lewo i prawo
    \item wybór przeciwników (groźny kandydat): 
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item pierwszy na lewo $c_L$ i pierwszy na prawo $c_R$ takich, że $score(c_?) \geq score(p)$
        \item powtarzamy dla kolejnych lewych $c_{L_{i}}$ takich, że $score(c_{L_{i}}) > score(c_{L_{i-1}})$
        \item analogicznie dla prawych $c_{R_{i}}$ takich, że $score(c_{R_{i}}) > score(c_{R_{i-1}})$
    \end{enumerate}
    \item usuwanie głosów - zaczynając od najbliższego prawego przeciwnika $c_R$ (dla lewych - odbicie lustrzane)
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item notacja $v$: $b(v)$ początek przedziału (lewy) i $e(v)$ koniec przedziały (prawy)
        \item wybieramy głosujących $\widehat{V}$ takich że $\{v \in V | \: b(v) \in (p,c_R]\}$
        \item sortujemy $\widehat{V}$ malejąco po $e(v)$ (od najbardziej prawego końca przedziału)
        \item usuwamy $scoreDelta(p, c_R) + 1$ głosów
    \end{enumerate}
    \item powtarzamy do skutu, przeliczając aprobaty co iterację
\end{enumerate}


\subsection{AV-CC-DV for Candidate Range = VI = column C1P = $r_v=0$}

Algorithm from \cite{control-cr-how-hard-magiera-faliszewski}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item notacja CR dla $c$: $v_{f(c)}$ początek przedziału (lewy/górny) i $v_{e(c)}$ koniec przedziału (prawy/dolny)
    \item kandydat $p$
    \item Głosujący - wybieramy głosujących $V' = \{v \in V | \: p \notin A(v)\}$ 
    \item Przeciwnicy -  wybieramy kandydatów $\widehat{C} = \{c \in C \setminus \{p\} | \: scoreDelta(c,p) \geq 0\}$
    \item Usuwanie głosów 
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item zaczynając od przeciwnika $c_N$ takiego, że $v_{e(c_N)}$ jest minimalne
        \item usuwamy $deltaScore(c_N,p)+1$ głosów, zaczynając od końca przedziału $v_{e(c_{N})}$
    \end{enumerate} 
    \item powtarzamy do skutu, przeliczając $V'$ i $\widehat{C}$ co iterację
\end{enumerate}

Notation used in the algorithm: \\
$x_a$, $r_a$ — point and radius \\ 
$b(a) = x_a - r_a$ — range beginning (left)\\
$e(a) = x_a + r_a$ — range end (right) \\
$p$ — candidate chosen to be a winner \\
$\widehat{C} = \{c \in C \setminus \{p\} | \: scoreDelta(c,p) \geq 0\}$ — opponents (dangerous candidates) \\
$V' = \{v \in V | \: p \notin A(v)\}$ — potential voters to delete \\

We use greedy strategy for voter deletion:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item We pick a leftmost opponent $c_L$ that is the one with the minimal $b(c_L)$
    \item We consider only voters who approve of $c_L$ that is, $\widehat{V} = \{v \in V' | \: c_L \in A(v)\}$
    \item We need to delete $deltaScore(c_L,p)+1$ rightmost voters from $\widehat{V}$
        that is the ones with the maximal $e(v)$, if it's not possible we fail
    \item We repeat the above steps until we succeed, recalculating $\widehat{C}$ and $\widehat{V}$ after each iteration
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Sketch Proof - CR case}
We will show how VCR algorithms is equivalent to the CR one.
Having voters in CR order, that is, they are still represented by a point $x_v$ on the $R$ line but have radius $0$.
Example in Figure \ref{fig:cr-cc-dv}.

\subsubsection{Choosing opponents}
In the CR case algorithm, we choose candidates to process, ordered by $v_{e(c)}$ ascending.
We can see that it is equivalent to picking the leftmost candidate $c_L$ from $\widehat{C}$ in the VCR ordering,
because it will also have the minimal $v_{e(c)}$, unless it fully contains some "smaller" candidate inside its interval.
We explain the special case below.

\subsubsection{Voter deletion}
Now, once we now how to pick an opponent, we will show that choosing voters is equivalent as well.

Notion of the beginning/end of the interval in the CR order is equivalent to the left/right in the VCR setting.
Both algorithms delete $scoreDelta(p,c) + 1$ voters,
CR one starts deleting voters from the end of interval.
VCR does virtually the same by deleting the rightmost voter.

\subsection{Sketch Proof - VR case}
We will show how VCR algorithms is equivalent to the VR one.
Having candidates in VR order, that is, they are still represented by a point $x_c$ on the $R$ line but have radius $0$.
Example in Figure \ref{fig:vr-cc-dv}.

\subsubsection{Choosing opponents}
VR algorithm picks dangerous candidates to the left and right of candidate $p$,
however later it proceeds only with defeating the right ones.
Once finished with them, it does the "mirror flip" to process candidates on the left side.
Even though the VCR algorithm picks leftmost candidate, we will show it is equivalent to the VR strategy
under some basic assumptions.

Following example in Figure \ref{fig:vr-cc-dv}, let's assume that candidate $p$ always will be the first candidate in the VR order.
Then we can see that VCR strategy of choosing leftmost candidate will be the same as VR algorithm choosing first on the right candidate.

This assumption is correct because, having candidates in order $[c_L, \: p, \: c_R]$,
if there was a voter $v$ who approved of $c_L$ and $c_R$ it would also have to approve of $p$ (because of VR property), so we could not delete $v$ anyway.

\subsubsection{Voter deletion}
Both algorithms have the same voter deletion strategy.
