\section{Conclusions} 
We have studied various problems, all centered around the one-dimensional Voter/Candidate Range preferences restriction.
First, in Chapter \ref{ch:detection} we proposed the VCR detection algorithm based on a reduction to ILP.
Our solution is perfectly suitable for basic analysis of the domain,
however, it lacks in the performance when it comes to recognizing larger profiles.
Next, in Chapter \ref{ch:domain} we performed extensive analysis of the VCR domain.
We showed that there is a significant number of profiles that satisfy the TVCR (i.e., neither VR nor CR) property.
This result is very promising, and encourages further development and research related to the VCR domain.
Finally, in Chapter \ref{ch:control} we showcase the advantages of preference restrictions.
That is, we demonstrate a polynomial-time algorithm for the constructive control by deleting voters (CC-DV) problem
for the VCR profiles, which in general is NP-Hard for the unrestricted profiles.
