Elections are ubiquitous element of our society in one form or another.
For example, choosing members of parliament or a winner of a sports competition,
in day-to-day activities, we need to schedule a meeting time, or a video streaming platform needs to provide recommended movies list to the user.
Each of these choices may require different type of election and different strategy of selecting the winner (or winners).
The differences come from the underlying properties and objectives of the choice.
For example, in the presidential election, we want to choose the candidate supported by the majority of the voters.
It is not the case for the parliamentary elections, where the goal is to select members of the parliament in such a way that 
the opinions of the voters are represented proportionally. 
See the work of \cite{faliszewskiSkowronMultiWinnerOverview}, which provides the suitable overview and discusses various election types.

\subsubsection{\textcolor{orange}{(WIP: Inny tytuł / Bez tytułu?)} Reseach objectives}
In this work we are going to focus on approval elections,
that is, on elections where each voter provides a subset of candidates that he or she approves of.


In general many social choice problems are known to be computationally hard.
However, they become tractable under some restrictions on the structure of the preferences.
\cite{DBLP:journals/corr/ElkindL15} explored well known restrictions in the setting of approval preferences,
introduced several new one-dimensional domain restriction models such as Voter Interval (VI) and Candidate Interval (CI),
based on the Dichotomous Euclidean restriction. 
\cite{falisz} generalized this model for the N-dimensional euclidean space calling it Voter Candidate Range (VCR)
and explored two-dimensional case.

The goal of this work is to explore properties of the one-dimensional model.
Search for the profile detection polynomial complexity algorithm. I will try as well to extend solution of \cite{mav}
for computing MAV score with polynomial complexity to the VCR model.
